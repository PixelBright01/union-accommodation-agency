About the Agency

The Union's Own Accommodation Agency

This platform exists because students were dealing with housing agents nobody could vouch for. The SUG Welfare Directorate decided to fix that — not by writing a warning, but by building its own agency, with its own registered agents, working directly for the Union's interest.

"An agent on this platform isn't just someone we checked — they're someone the Union itself stands behind."

How It Works

What Approval Actually Covers

An agent doesn't simply sign up. Each application goes through a structured process before it's allowed to go public.

  • Full identity, business name, and contact details collected
  • NIN slip (individual) or CAC document (business) reviewed
  • Matric number and department confirmed for Student Agents
  • Reviewed first by the SUG Director of Welfare
  • Final joint sign-off from the Office of the President
