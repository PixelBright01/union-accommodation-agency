Why It's Different

Backed by the Union, Not Just "Verified"

Three things that separate this from any informal agent you'd meet off the street.

✓

Dual Sign-Off Approval

Every agent is approved by both the SUG Director of Welfare and the Office of the President before they're allowed to operate.

🛡

About the Portal

The Union's Own Digital Platform

This platform exists because students were dealing with housing agents and transport operators nobody could vouch for. The SUG Welfare Directorate decided to fix that — not by writing a warning, but by building its own system, with its own registered agents and drivers, working directly for the Union's interest.

"An agent on this platform isn't just someone we checked — they're someone the Union itself stands behind."

How It Works

What Approval Actually Covers

An agent or driver doesn't simply sign up. Each application goes through a structured process before it's allowed to go public.

  • Full identity, business name, and contact details collected
  • NIN slip (individual) or CAC document (business) reviewed
  • Matric number and department confirmed for Student Agents
  • Reviewed first by the SUG Director of Welfare
  • Final joint sign-off from the Office of the President
